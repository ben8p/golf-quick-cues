DRIVER
The Upward Strike

1. SETUP (The Power Tilt)

  • Ball Position: Opposite the inside of the lead heel.
  • Body: Aggressively tilt the spine/shoulders back (away from target). Back shoulder visibly lower.
  • Weight: Feel 60/40 on the back foot.
  • Tee Height: Half the ball above the crown.

2. SWING THOUGHT (The Sweep)

  • Eyes: Focused on the back half of the ball.
  • Tempo: Smooth, wide takeaway for max width.
  • Goal: SWEEP the ball off the tee (upward strike).
  • Finish: Full, balanced follow-through.

3. MISTAKES TO AVOID

  • DO NOT shift weight to the front on the backswing.
  • DO NOT lunge forward on the downswing.
  • DO NOT lift your head early (keep eyes down).

HYBRID
The Shallow Strike

1. SETUP (The Centered Stance)

  • Ball Position: Opposite the inside of the lead heel (same as driver).
  • Body: Shoulders and spine only slightly tilted back (less than the driver).
  • Weight: Centered (‚àº50/50) or just a touch forward.

2. SWING THOUGHT (The Glide)

  • Eyes: Focused on the back half of the ball.
  • Tempo: Smooth, controlled rhythm.
  • Goal: SHALLOW strike to glide the sole along the turf (level impact).
  • Action: Trust the loft and the long, controlled swing.

3. MISTAKES TO AVOID

  • DO NOT try to hit up on the ball too much.
  • DO NOT let the ball creep back in the stance (causes a steep, digging strike).
  • DO NOT rush the downswing.

MID-IRON (7-IRON)
Consistency

1. SETUP (Consistency)

  • Stance: Feet about shoulder-width, all lines (feet, hips, shoulders) parallel to the target.
  • Ball: Slightly forward of center (opposite your chest/sternum).
  • Weight: Centered (50/50).
  • Shaft: Lean the shaft slightly forward so hands are ahead of the ball.

2. SWING THOUGHT (The Strike)

  • Tempo: Smooth and controlled, trust your rhythm.
  • Contact Goal: Ball-first, then turf! (Focus on brushing the grass after the ball).
  • Power: Rotate your body (shoulders and hips) through impact.

3. MISTAKES TO AVOID

  • DO NOT try to lift or scoop the ball (you'll hit the ground first).
  • DO NOT rush the tempo and lose balance.
  • Check: Is the ball too far back? (Avoid a low, digging shot).

LONG PITCH
The Feel

1. SETUP (The Feel)

  • Stance: Feet slightly wider than normal (hips-width).
  • Ball Position: Forward of center (opposite chest/logo).
  • Weight: Slightly favoring the lead foot (‚àº55/45).
  • Club: Standard Pitch: Square face. High Trajectory: Open the face before gripping.

2. SWING THOUGHT (The Rhythm)

  • Tempo: Smooth, controlled backswing. Maintain rhythm through impact.
  • Action: Allow the wrists to hinge slightly for trajectory and spin.
  • Goal: Swing with commitment and speed‚Äîdo not decelerate!

3. MISTAKES TO AVOID

  • DO NOT over-swing and lose control of the club.
  • DO NOT decelerate into impact (speed is your friend).
  • DO NOT try to scoop or lift the ball (let the loft do the work).

SHORT PITCH
Control/Low Trajectory

1. SETUP (The Stack)

  • Stance: Very narrow (‚àº1 clubhead wide). Front foot slightly open.
  • Ball Position: Very far back (opposite trail heel/back foot).
  • Weight: Aggressively 80/20 on the lead foot.
  • Shaft: Hands pressed far forward (shaft leaning toward target).

2. SWING THOUGHT (The Strike)

  • Focus: Maintain that forward weight through the entire swing.
  • Action: Swing with mostly arms and shoulders; control over power.
  • Goal: A sharp, descending strike for crisp contact.

3. MISTAKES TO AVOID

  • DO NOT fall back or try to scoop the ball.
  • DO NOT release or "flip" the wrists early.
  • Check: Is the ball too far forward? (Avoid thin or fat contact).

BUNKER
The Explosion

1. SETUP (The Foundation)

  • Clubface: WIDE OPEN (it must point up and slightly right of target) before gripping.
  • Feet: Dig them in wide for stability and to gauge the sand.
  • Stance: Open stance (feet aimed left of the target).
  • Weight: Heavily 70/30 on the lead foot. Keep it there!

2. SWING THOUGHT (The Explosion)

  • Target: Aim to hit the sand 2‚àí3 inches behind the ball.
  • Action: Swing with full acceleration and commitment.
  • Goal: Let the club glide through the sand. Finish high with the club.

3. MISTAKES TO AVOID

  • DO NOT decelerate or stop at impact (that leaves the ball in the bunker).
  • DO NOT strike the ball first (causes it to fly over the green).
  • Check: Is the clubface closed? (Avoid a thin, hot shot).

BASIC CHIP
Putter-Like

1. SETUP (The Stability)

  • Grip: Choke down for control.
  • Feet: Narrow stance. Hips/feet slightly open to the target.
  • Ball Position: Back in the stance (closer to the trail foot/heel).
  • Weight: Heavily 70/30 on the lead foot. Keep it there.

2. SWING THOUGHT (The Motion)

  • Action: Swing the arms back and through, like a long, smooth putt.
  • Body: Quiet lower body. Swing with arms and shoulders only (keep the triangle connected).
  • Wrists: Keep them firm‚Äîminimal to no hinge.

3. MISTAKES TO AVOID

  • DO NOT flip or release the wrists at impact.
  • DO NOT shift your weight backward during the swing.
  • DO NOT use too much power; focus on rhythm and tempo.

STANDARD PUTT
The Pendulum

1. SETUP (The Stillness)

  • Grip: Light and relaxed pressure.
  • Eyes: Directly over the ball (use the "drop test").
  • Stance: Narrow for control; square to the target line.
  • Ball Position: Just forward of center (to catch it slightly on the upswing).
  • Weight: Evenly distributed (50/50).

2. STROKE THOUGHT (The Pendulum)

  • Motion: A smooth, pendulum stroke driven by the rocking of the shoulders.
  • Unit: Arms, shoulders, and putter move as one unit.
  • Tempo: Even pace; accelerate gently through the ball.

3. MISTAKES TO AVOID

  • DO NOT flip or break the wrists instead of rocking the shoulders.
  • DO NOT have a jerky or rushed stroke.
  • DO NOT look up too soon‚Äîkeep your head still until you hear the ball drop.
